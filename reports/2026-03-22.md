Overall traffic is healthy and trending upward, with impressions and clicks both growing week-on-week across a broad range of pages. ## 🔴 Urgent Actions (do this week)

**1.**
- **Problem:** `https://www.ahurucandles.co.nz/blogs/guide/best-essential-oils-for-diffusers-guide` — 199,728 impressions over 90 days with a 0.33% CTR. This is the single largest traffic opportunity on the entire site and it is being almost entirely wasted. The page is ranking at an average position of 4.7 — well within clicking range — but searchers are not choosing it. The title and description are almost certainly generic or truncated, giving searchers no reason to click over competitors.
- **Fix:** Update the Shopify blog post meta title and meta description immediately. Use these exact strings:
  - **Title:** `Best Essential Oils for Diffusers — Complete NZ Guide`
  - **Description:** `Discover which essential oils work best in your diffuser. NZ-made blends, recipes for relaxation, sleep & focus. Pure essential oils, no synthetics. Shop Āhuru.`
- **Why now:** At position 4.7, even lifting CTR from 0.33% to 2% would deliver roughly 13× more clicks from existing rankings — no new content or link building required. This is the fastest possible win on the site.

**2.**
- **Problem:** `https://www.ahurucandles.co.nz/products/meri-kirihimete-christmas-card` — 44,214 impressions over 90 days with a CTR of just 0.04% (18 clicks). The query "meri kirihimete" alone has 34,265 impressions and is the second-highest impression query in the dataset. This page appears to be ranking for a high-volume Māori language search term but the product (a Christmas card) is out of season in March and the listing is almost certainly not answering searcher intent — people searching this phrase are looking for greetings content, not a product page for a card.
- **Fix:** This page should not be the primary ranking page for this query. Add a canonical tag pointing to a more appropriate page if one exists, or suppress this page from indexing temporarily given it is out of season. More importantly, create a short editorial blog post optimised for "meri kirihimete" that provides genuine greeting content and links to relevant products. This moves the ranking to a page that can actually earn clicks year-round. In the meantime, update the product page meta description to at minimum capture seasonal traffic when it returns: `Meri Kirihimete — send aromatic Christmas wishes with our handcrafted NZ gift card. Perfect with an Āhuru candle or essential oil gift box.`
- **Why now:** 34,265 impressions for a single query with 11 clicks (0.03% CTR) is the second-biggest missed opportunity in the dataset. Even a modest improvement in CTR would deliver significant click volume.
